# Ethics Statement — WriteLens Adaptive Writing Assessment System

## 1. Context

This study was conducted at the University of Ain Temouchent (Algeria) within the course **Academic Writing** (Course ID: 379), taught by instructor **Fatima GUERCH**. The study focuses on third-year EFL undergraduate students during the academic period **November 2025 – March 2026**.

---

## 2. Purpose

The WriteLens system analyses student writing behaviour and writing quality to provide adaptive, formative feedback. It is designed to **assist instructors** in understanding learner development — it does not automate grading or replace teacher judgment.

---

## 3. Data Sources Used

| Data Type | Source | Use |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Moodle activity logs | Moodle platform (course 379) | Engagement indicators |
| Rubric scores | Instructor-assessed | Writing quality dimensions |
| Essay texts | Student submissions (Draft 1 & Draft 2) | Text analytics |
| Chat/help messages | Moodle messaging | Help-seeking behaviour |

All data was accessed through the course Learning Management System (LMS) under the supervision of the course instructor.

---

## 4. Informed Consent

- Students were informed that their anonymised activity data and writing samples would be used for educational research purposes.
- Participation in this study did not affect academic grades.
- The focal case study student (**Asmaa L., User ID 9263**) provided explicit consent for use of her learning traces in this academic study.
- All data is stored locally and is not shared with third parties.

---

## 5. Anonymity and Data Protection

- Student names are used only for internal case identification within a secure research environment.
- No personally identifiable information is published in the thesis.
- All stored data files are password-protected and will be deleted after the thesis defence.

---

## 6. Institutional Oversight

This research is conducted in partial fulfilment of the requirements for a Master's or Doctoral degree at the University of Ain Temouchent and adheres to the institution's research ethics guidelines. Approval for access to LMS data was granted by the course instructor.

---

## 7. Researcher Commitment

The researcher commits to:
- Using data solely for the stated research purpose
- Not sharing or publishing individual student data
- Providing access to results to participants upon request
- Deleting all personal data upon completion of the study
